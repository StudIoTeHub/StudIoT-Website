Arsenic Biosensor

In collaboration with PhD candidate Martin Pothier out of a University of Ottawa microbiology and geochemistry lab, we are creating a device that is entirely open sourced. Using only off-the-shelf parts such as Arduinos, LED and photo-diodes, the device will be able to tell the user, with accuracy, the concentration of arsenic in the sampled drinking water.

Keywords: Citizen Science, Open-Source Hardware, Biosensing

Urban Farming and IoT

As part of an exploritory Internet of Things in agriculture project, we are currently monitoring the vitals of a small cherry tomato plant. Using open-source hardware, sensors and IoT platforms, we can track the soil moisture, room temperature and humidity, exposure to light and even automatically water the plant all through one web application.

Keywords: AgTech, Open-Source Hardware, Internet of Things

IoT Curriculum

An initiative we have taken on is to reach out to students at the University of Ottawa that have heard of the Internet of Things and really spark an interest in the domain. We have held talks and meetups about IoT and we paln on hosting workshops, bootcamps and possibly a Hack-a-thon in the future. The goal is to generate talent in IoT.

Keywords: Internet of Things, Education, Skills
